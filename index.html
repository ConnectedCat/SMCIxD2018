The Future of Mobility

Tarelle Butts

Situated at the intersection of technology, game/environmental/service design, the Future of Mobility provides solution-oriented experiences for the everyday commuter.

The Future of Mobility

In a city struggling with pollution, urban sprawl and traffic overload public transportation should be able to provide a variety of solutions in improving urban dweller’s lives. And yet most people who have taken subways and busses can attest to the subpar quality of the experience.

“The Future of Mobility” envisions the possibilities of public transportation in becoming not just a means of commute but also a means to improve the quality of life of the passengers. The project is situated on the intersection of technology, game design, environmental design, and service design. Through rigorous design research we’ve identified the key points of intervention where quality improvements would be most prominent. We have also outlined the measures to be taken and their prospective impact on the commuter’s experience.

This exhibition presents our process in coming up with these solutions as well as our proposals of how they can be implemented along with the design for the relevant artifacts.

ReStitch

In partnership with SAP we’ve reimagined the thrift store experience.

ReStitch is an omnichannel clothing resale solution that pairs intelligent software with IoT hardware to optimize the process of buying, selling, and shopping for secondhand clothes. With our system, thrifting is no longer just a brick and mortar affair—now you can sell your unwanted clothes and browse the racks of your favorite thrift store right from your phone. Our streamlined system of smart software and hardware revolutionizes thrift store operations and passes on the benefits to the shopper.

We invite you to try it for yourself and experience a new way to thrift.

Moldable Interactions

Heidi Gaudet

In response to uninspired, static hand gestures Moldable Interactions seeks to create a familiar yet surprising experience with the tactile controllers on the intersection between experimentation and innovation.

Moldable Interactions

As a reaction to the ubiquity of uninspired, static hand gestures, Heidi Gaudet created (Unusual) Moldable Interactions. Her material research led her to play and think about how interfaces could be reimagined as expressive, moldable surfaces. Her early experiments included the study of sensory experiences aroused by Jell-O, agar, and slime. When paired with a visual and/or physical output (triggered by a pressure sensor embedded in slime), these malleable substances produce a feeling of deep satisfaction and agency. The experimental nature gives the sense of being able to manipulate a previously inaccessible surface/dimension

Alongside Artificial Knowing, Heidi participated in performative prototyping, from which the idea of expressive interactions was born. Next came the creation of two additional controls: the flex sensor and arm movement.

From the hardware to the interface, Heidi sought to create an experience which felt at once familiar yet surprising and could ground the tactile controllers. Her intent with these objects is to find the meeting point between experimentation and innovation. After graduation she will continue to apply this process to the exploration of new interface models.

ALEYE: Connect with your Heart

Maile Malin

Shortlisted for the 2018 IxDA awards, ALEYE is a wearable heart rate and EKG monitor allowing the wearer and their loved ones to monitor their heart's activity through a mobile app.

ALEYE: Connect with your Heart

In 2015 my mom suffered a heart attack while at work. Not knowing what the pain was from, she tried working through it, waiting much too long to get help. Luckily a co-worker insisted on driving her to the hospital and she survived. Now she regularly worries if, or when, she might have another heart attack.

My mom is not alone. 30% of the 15 million heart attack survivors in the US have the same concern as my mom, and now suffer from anxiety or depression. Panic leads to feelings of a heart attack, which leads to more anxiety, which makes their panic attacks worse. Another survivor I interviewed said it makes him feel better to see what his heart is doing. This led me to the concept of a wearable heart rate and EKG monitor which connects the wearer to their heart's activity and their loved ones to them in the case of an emergency through a mobile app, which communicates with the wearable. This became ALEYE.

ALEYE: Connect with your Heart was shortlisted for the 2018 IxDA awards in the Connecting category

Charlie. An Inclusive Assistant.

Ojen Shammasian

Charlie is a cloud-based personalized assistant that seeks to eliminate people’s dependency on physical interactions and visual interfaces in order to make modern technology accessible to everyone.

Charlie. An Inclusive Assistant

A project that explores intuitive and effective methods to use AI to eliminate people’s dependency on physical interactions and visual interfaces. Today, with technologies like Siri, Alexa, and Google Assistant, voice interactions support thousands of people in their day-to-day. You can ask Alexa to dim the lights while you browse through Netflix, and Siri can update you on your morning commute while you pour yourself a cup of coffee. But what if you weren’t physically able to use a remote control, or get a brew going for that matter? Current voice technologies lack standalone data connectivity, smart remote control, and intelligent adaptation to speech impairment. Charlie, on the other hand, is a cloud based personalized assistant that syncs to all your devices and adjusts itself depending on who you are and what you need.

By weaving inclusion and technology together, Charlie makes anything possible for everyone.

INSTA-FACT. Truth must travel faster.

Ana Massette

Insta-Fact is a system that analyzes news to determine if what people are listening to or watching is truthful or not by examining the validity of the sources.

INSTA-FACT. Truth must travel faster

In the modern world we live in, decision making process is predominantly influenced by what we see and hear on news sources. However, very often, those decisions can be misguided by suspect or even intentionally misleading sources.

With that in mind, I conducted intense research to develop a project that would allow people to be honestly informed, in real time, by news sources rigorously vetted. Insta-Fact, a system that recognizes and analyzes news sources and lets people know if what they are listening to or watching is truthful or not by scouring all available sources on the internet to determine the validity of the source. Since there is no such thing as completely true or false, the system uses color codes along with a percentage scale to let people know the relative validity of the source: Red = 00% - 49% truth = FAKE | Yellow = 50% - 89% truth = BIASED | Green = 90% - 100% truth = FACT.

Once people are no longer being manipulated by false information, truthful social impact is formed where decisions are being made based solely on facts, which Impart knowledge and ultimately creates a better society.

hush. be present.

Mike Sonnenberg

Hush redefines our relationships with technology. It's a hybrid smartwatch that enables greater control over the way mobile notifications are received, creating a more focused and mindful lifestyle.

hush. be present

Hush is a hybrid smartwatch that enables greater control over the way mobile notifications are received, creating a more focused and mindful lifestyle. It was inspired by an exploration of our relationships with technology. I questioned how those relationships are defined and who (or what) defines them.

Months of user interviews, prototyping, testing and iteration resulted in a system that places the user in control of the relationships they have with their devices. Hush allows users to limit the amount of mental bandwidth their devices are able to capture while remaining useful as tools of communication. It acknowledges that the relevance of specific communications and the desire to receive them is non-binary and changes throughout the day.

Tita’s Potluck. Still.

Petra Nichols

Two projects presented, Tita’s Potluck and Still enable people to connect with each other and empower people to gain control over their personal noise level surroundings.

Tita’s Potluck. Still.

Human empathy and mutual support is something we look for in our social interactions and something that can be obscured by a layer of technology which modern life places between us. Making technology transparent and helping humans re-establish their connectedness is at the forefront of our design inquiry.

The first piece, “Tita’s Potluck”, is an app meant to facilitate the activities and interactions around a once-common but now disappearing practice of hosting and organizing a shared meal for a group of friends. The goal is to bring forward the human element intrinsic to our social interactions and to help people connect without being burdened.

“Still” is smart wearable tasked with bringing awareness to the hearing loss conditions and human empathy to the spread of this problem. By giving our users the tools to indicate dangerous levels of sound pollution we hope to help them gain control of their personal noise surroundings and take measures to protect their hearing, but also to spread awareness of how common this danger is and how many people suffer from this condition.
